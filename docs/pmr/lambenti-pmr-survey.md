# Home Lighting and Living Space Survey — Lambenti PMR Filter

Status: paste-ready PMR survey and operational analysis plan.
Default currency: CAD. If distributing in the United States, clone the form and replace CAD with USD; do not mix currencies in one unbranched survey.
Estimated completion time: 5–8 minutes.
Design rule: behavior and household questions come before the Lambenti concept test to reduce concept-induced bias.
Option-format rule: every multiple-choice/multi-select option is lettered A–Z as requested.

## Recommended Question Order

A. Household and living situation — Q1–Q4.
B. Decorating authority and purchase decision-making — Q5–Q8.
C. Aesthetic importance — Q9–Q12.
D. Lighting purchase history — Q13–Q17.
E. Where people buy lighting — Q18–Q19.
F. Spending behavior — Q20–Q22.
G. Lambenti concept test — Q23–Q30.
H. Segmentation/scoring — calculated after export, not shown to respondents.

## Form Instructions for Respondents

This survey asks about home lighting, decor, and living-space preferences. It should take about 5–8 minutes. There are no right or wrong answers.

# Segment Rules

## Strong Lambenti Prospect
A. Total score 70+.
B. Q23 interest is 6 or 7.
C. Has control over at least one relevant space.
D. Would consider purchase at $80+ CAD.

These are likely early customers.

## Medium Lambenti Prospect
A. Total score 50–69.
B. Q23 interest is 5+.
C. Has some aesthetic priority or relevant lighting behavior.

These may convert with a stronger demo, lower price, better proof, or clearer use case.

## Weak Lambenti Prospect
A. Total score 25–49.
B. Low direct interest or mostly utility-only lighting behavior.

They may appreciate the idea but are not obvious early customers.

## Poor Fit / Not Current Target
A. Total score below 25.
B. Low aesthetic priority.
C. No relevant space control.
D. No lighting behavior.
E. No Lambenti interest.

Do not prioritize.

## Unproven but Interesting
A. Has not bought lighting in the past 24 months.
B. Q9 aesthetic score is 8–10.
C. Has control over desk, bedroom, TV/media, gaming, shelf/display, living room, or entire home.
D. Q23 interest is 6 or 7.

Do not eliminate this group prematurely. They may represent concept-created demand if a demo is strong.

# Recommended Minimum Sample Size

A. Fast directional read: 80–100 completed responses. Use only for early signal detection; do not overfit subsegments.
B. Practical PMR filter target: 150–250 completed responses. This is enough to compare broad groups such as atmosphere buyers vs utility-only buyers, assuming the sample has enough lighting buyers.
C. Better segmented read: 300+ completed responses, especially if splitting by desk/gaming/TV/bedroom use cases, gender/age/source/channel, or Canada vs United States.
D. Minimum per segment before trusting a segment rate: about 30 respondents in that segment. Treat any segment with N < 30 as directional only.

# Initial Launch Target Recommendation

Target first: aesthetic-control buyers who already improve desks, gaming setups, TV/media areas, bedrooms, or shelves and have bought LED strips, desk/monitor lamps, TV/gaming backlighting, smart lighting, mood/ambient lighting, modular panels, or DIY lighting parts.

Why:

A. They already understand that lighting can transform a space.
B. They are more likely to tolerate cable management and stick-on setup.
C. They are more likely to value hidden control, tactile novelty, and a premium physical object.
D. They are more likely to compare Lambenti against LED strips, smart lamps, monitor bars, Nanoleaf-style panels, TV backlights, and desk/gaming accessories rather than against cheap replacement bulbs.
E. Their purchase behavior can validate price-qualified interest near the $80–149 CAD launch zone.

Second target: high-aesthetic respondents with relevant space control but no recent lighting purchase, if Q23 is 6–7 and Q25 mentions beauty, mood, novelty, premium feel, or satisfying interaction. This group is unproven but could indicate Lambenti creates demand beyond current lighting buyers.

Do not target first: utility-only replacement-bulb/fixture/security-light buyers with low aesthetic priority or limited decorating authority, even if they find the concept mildly interesting.

# Recommended First Landing-Page / Ad Message to Test

A. Primary angle: “A hidden ambient light you shape by touch.”
B. Secondary angle: “No visible switch. No remote. No app. Just a warm backlight and a small magnetic object on your desk, shelf, or media stand.”
C. Product proof needed: short demo video showing the wooden hexagon changing brightness smoothly through a desk/shelf surface.
D. Objection proof needed: setup/cable management, premium actuator/materials, surface compatibility, and reliability.
